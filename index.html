Token liquidity in the capital markets refers to the speed with which an investment can be exchanged without adversely affecting its valuation. Liquid assets are quickly sold at fair market value or current market price. As a result, assets with more liquidity are sold at a higher price. However, in cryptocurrencies, the term liquidity is defined as the ability of an asset to be easily converted into cash or coins. Liquidity is important for all tradable assets, including cryptocurrencies. A low level of liquidity indicates that market volatility is present, leading to price fluctuations of the cryptocurrency. High liquidity shows stable demand and little price fluctuation. Furthermore, high liquidity is an optimal situation as it leads to improved prices for all parties involved. A thriving economy with a high level of trading activity offers a desirable market price for everyone.
